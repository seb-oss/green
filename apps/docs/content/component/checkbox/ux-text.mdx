## Labels
The same text principles can be used for labels in input fields, radio buttons, checkboxes, dropdowns,  headings, page titles, navigation texts, link texts, and other short texts.

Write checkbox labels so customers can easily understand what checking or leaving the box blank means. This is especially important for standalone checkboxes, such as accepting terms or subscribing to emails with news and offers. It’s also crucial when a setting needs to be turned on or off, and the user must actively make a choice. (If most users would choose the same default option, use a toggle instead.)

Make sure the label and options match. If the field label is a yes or no question, the answer options should be “Yes” and “No” – nothing else.
 
Keep options consistent in style. Choose one of the following approaches and stick to it:
- Full sentences starting with a capital letter and ending with a full stop.
- A single flowing sentence where only the last option ends with a full stop.
- Short phrases or single words without a full stop.


## Avoid negations and use the “I” form
Avoid using negations like “Do not send me more emails” in checkbox labels, as this forces users to check the box to prevent something from happening. Also, avoid questions like “Do you want to receive our newsletter?” Instead, follow the convention of using the “I” form for checkboxes.

## Accepting the terms
When asking users to accept terms, use the “I” form and avoid phrasing it as a question in an individual checkbox. A question can be used in the header label.

### Do
<Grid columns="2" gap="m">
    <div>
        <SE>Jag har läst informationen och granskat de uppgifter jag fyllt I. Jag försäkrar att de är riktiga.</SE>
        <EN>I have read the information and reviewed what I entered in the form. I confirm that everything is correct.</EN>
    </div>
    <Figma type="do" id="accepting-do" caption="Use the 'I' form" />
</Grid>

### Don’t
<Grid columns="2" gap="m">
    <div>
        <SE>Har du läst informationen och granskat de uppgifter du fyllt i? Försäkrar du att de är riktiga?</SE>
        <EN>Have you read the information and reviewed what was entered in the form? Can you confirm that everything is correct?</EN>
    </div>
    <Figma type="dont" id="accepting-dont" caption="Use the 'I' form" />
</Grid>

## Multiple options
Decide on a consistent style for option labels:
- Full sentences starting with a capital letter and ending with a full stop.
- A continuous sentence starting with lowercase, with only the last option ending in a full stop.
- Single words or short phrases with no full stop

### Do
- Provide clear instructions so users know they can select multiple options.
- Ensure the label and options match. If the field label is a yes/no question, the answer options should be “Yes” and “No” – nothing else.
